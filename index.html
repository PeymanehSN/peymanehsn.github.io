About
I build at the edge of behavior, markets, and systems.

I am a behavioral economist and founder with a PhD in Behavioral Economics from EBS University of Business and Law in Germany.

My doctoral research examined how social comparisons, incentives, and peer effects shape contractual and organizational design. I also hold an MSc in Environmental Economics, where I studied political incentives in international environmental agreements.

How can better systems change behavior when value is hidden, incentives are misaligned, or decisions feel too costly?

That question has shaped my work across organizational economics, environmental economics, behavioral finance, sustainability, policy design, and consumer decision-making.

I do not see entrepreneurship only as building products. I see it as designing better decision environments.

Founder Thesis
The best products do not only digitize behavior. They redesign decisions.

Many products assume people will act once they have information. My work starts from a different observation: people often delay, avoid, or misjudge decisions because the environment around the decision is poorly designed.

Invisible value

People misjudge what they already have.

When value is hidden, people delay action, underuse assets, or fail to make better choices.

Decision friction

The next step is often too unclear.

People may want to act, but uncertainty, effort, and low trust make action feel costly.

Product opportunity

Better systems can make action natural.

My founder edge is translating behavioral economics into products that reduce friction and create action.

I am drawn to problems where value exists, but action does not happen.
Founder Work

Current Venture

BAVO

BAVO is my current venture at the intersection of behavioral economics, AI-enabled product design, sustainability, and consumer decision-making.

The venture began from a behavioral insight: ownership is one of the most under-designed parts of consumer life. People often own valuable things they rarely use, but they lack systems that help them see that value, understand it, and act on it.

My role has been to identify the market thesis, shape the behavioral wedge, assemble the founding team, lead early validation, and define the product direction from consumer tool to ownership intelligence infrastructure.

Previous Venture

MindMeld Money

MindMeld Money was an experimental fintech platform applying game theory and behavioral economics to investor decision-making.

I designed user research, surveys, focus groups, and behavioral experiments with early users. The work explored how interactive scenarios and gamified simulations could make financial learning more engaging than traditional advisory formats.

This project helped me move from theory to product: from research insight, to hypothesis, to prototype, to user feedback.

Why I Build
I am drawn to problems where value exists, but action does not happen.

People often fail to act because information is missing, incentives are unclear, trust is weak, or the next step feels too costly.

That is where behavioral economics becomes product strategy.

For me, entrepreneurship is about designing systems that make better decisions easier, more valuable, and more natural.

This is the thread connecting my research, advisory work, MindMeld Money, and my current venture.

The strongest products do not simply explain decisions. They help people make better ones.
Beyond Work
Culture shapes how I think about people.

Poetry, literature, painting, and culture remind me that decisions are not only rational calculations.

They are tied to identity, memory, emotion, aspiration, and context. That is why I am drawn to products that sit close to real life: how people choose, keep, compare, spend, save, hesitate, and act.
