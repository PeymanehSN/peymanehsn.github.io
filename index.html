About
I build at the edge of behavior, markets, and systems.

I am a behavioral economist and founder with a PhD in Behavioral Economics from EBS University of Business and Law in Germany.

My doctoral research examined how social comparisons shape contractual and organizational design, with a focus on incentives, peer effects, and workplace behavior.

How can better systems change behavior when value is hidden, incentives are misaligned, or decisions feel too costly?

That question has shaped my work across organizational economics, environmental economics, behavioral finance, sustainability, policy design, and consumer decision-making.

I am especially interested in building systems that turn invisible value into action.

MindMeld Money was an experimental fintech platform applying game theory and behavioral economics to investor education and financial decision-making.

Founder edge

Why I Build
I am drawn to problems where value exists, but action does not happen.

People often fail to act because information is missing, incentives are unclear, trust is weak, or the next step feels too costly.

That is where behavioral economics becomes product strategy.

For me, entrepreneurship is about designing systems that make better decisions easier, more valuable, and more natural.

This is the thread connecting my research, advisory work, MindMeld Money, and my current venture.
