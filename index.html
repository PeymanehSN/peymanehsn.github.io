About

I build where behavior meets markets.

Many products fail not because the technology is impossible, but because the decision moment is misunderstood.

From research to venture building

My academic work studied how incentives, social comparison, and institutional design shape decisions inside organizations and markets. Over time, I became increasingly interested in taking those insights beyond research and turning them into products that help people make better decisions in real life.

That path led me into entrepreneurship: first through MindMeld Money, an experimental fintech platform applying behavioral economics and game theory to investor decision-making, and now through BAVO, a venture focused on the missing ownership layer in fashion.

People do not act just because information exists. They act when value becomes visible, personal, and easy to use.
Current Venture

BAVO

The ownership layer for fashion.

Fashion built the engine for buying. BAVO is building the engine for owning.

BAVO turns wardrobes into priced systems of action. Users scan their wardrobe, see item-level value, detect dormant pieces, and receive clear next steps: sell, restyle, donate, repair, recycle, or keep.

Most people do not know what their wardrobe is worth or what to do with unused items. BAVO makes that invisible value visible and actionable.
