About

Behavioral economics as a founder edge.

I work on problems where people have options, but still do not act because the system around the decision is poorly designed.

From research depth to company building.

I have a PhD in Behavioral Economics from EBS University of Business and Law, where I studied how social comparisons, incentives, and peer effects shape contractual and organizational design. I also hold an MSc in Environmental Economics, where I studied political incentives in international environmental agreements.

My path has moved from academic research to founder work, but the central question has stayed the same: how do we design systems that help people make better decisions when value is hidden, incentives are misaligned, or action feels costly?

I am drawn to problems where value already exists, but the right decision system does not.

Founder Work

Two ventures, one thread: better decisions.

My founder work connects behavioral insight with product experimentation, market design, and zero-to-one learning.

BAVO

BAVO is my current venture at the intersection of behavioral economics, AI-enabled product design, sustainability, and consumer decision-making.

The venture began from a behavioral insight: ownership is one of the most under-designed parts of consumer life. People often own valuable things they rarely use, but they lack systems that help them see that value, understand it, and act on it.

My role includes identifying the market thesis, shaping the behavioral wedge, assembling the founding team, leading early validation, defining sustainability logic, and building the roadmap from consumer tool to ownership intelligence infrastructure.

Behavioral decision design Sustainable consumption Consumer ownership Valuation logic Circular economy

MindMeld Money

Before BAVO, I founded MindMeld Money, an experimental fintech platform applying behavioral economics and game theory to investor decision-making.

I designed user research, surveys, focus groups, behavioral experiments, and early product concepts to test how people respond to risk, incentives, and interactive financial learning.

MindMeld Money taught me how to move from theory to product: from research insight, to hypothesis, to prototype, to user feedback.
