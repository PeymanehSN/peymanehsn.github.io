About

I build where behavior meets markets.

My work focuses on products where the hard question is not only what can be built, but what makes people trust, use, and act.

From behavioral research to venture building

My academic work studied how incentives, social comparison, and institutional design shape decisions inside organizations and markets. Over time, I became increasingly interested in taking those insights beyond research and turning them into products that change real behavior.

I first built MindMeld Money, an experimental fintech platform applying behavioral economics and game theory to investor decision-making. Today, I am building BAVO, a company focused on the missing ownership layer in fashion.

My founder edge is understanding the decision moment: what people delay, what they overvalue, what they ignore, and what finally makes them act.
Current venture

BAVO

The ownership layer for fashion.

Fashion built the engine for buying. BAVO is building the engine for owning.

BAVO turns wardrobes into priced systems of action. Users scan their wardrobe, see item-level value, identify dormant pieces, and receive clear next steps: sell, restyle, donate, repair, recycle, or keep.

The insight is simple: most people do not need another closet organizer. They need a valuation and action system that makes hidden wardrobe value visible enough to trigger action.
